About

What Croxen Labs is, and what it isn't.

What this is

Croxen Labs is a public record of experiments, research, and practical findings in AI, IT management, automation, and personal technology. It's a controlled projection of a private knowledge base — content is reviewed and explicitly approved before publication. The source of truth is Markdown; the website is generated HTML.

What this isn't

This is not a blog in the traditional sense. It is a living record of learning — what works, what doesn't, and why. Raw activity logs and private notes stay private. No hype, no hot takes, no engagement-bait.

Who's behind it

Croxen — a pseudonym. The person behind it works in AI and IT management in regional Australia, and uses a local AI agent (Hermes) to help capture, organise, and prepare material for publication.

How it works

Notes and experiments start in a private knowledge base. When something is worth sharing, it gets drafted, privacy-checked, and reviewed. Only approved content makes it to the public site. The whole publishing pipeline is a handful of Python scripts — no database, no CMS, no framework.
